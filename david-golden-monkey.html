About the Golden Snub-Nosed Monkey

The Golden Snub-Nosed Monkey (Rhinopithecus roxellana) is one of China's most magnificent and endangered primates, known for its striking golden fur and distinctive upturned nose. These remarkable monkeys are perfectly adapted to life in the cold, high-altitude forests of central China, where they form complex social groups and play a crucial role in forest ecosystems.

With their thick, luxurious golden coats, expressive blue faces, and unique social structures, Golden Snub-Nosed Monkeys are not only beautiful but also highly intelligent creatures that have captured the hearts of researchers and conservationists worldwide. They serve as important flagship species for mountain forest conservation in China.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Rhinopithecus roxellana
  • Common Names: Golden Snub-Nosed Monkey, Sichuan Golden Monkey
  • Size: 51-83cm body
  • Weight: 15-39 kg
  • Lifespan: 20-25 years
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Habitat: Mountain forests
  • Activity: Diurnal
  • Conservation: Endangered
